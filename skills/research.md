# Research

## When to use
Hypothesis-driven exploration, opportunity sizing, "what if" questions.

## Methodology
1. State the hypothesis or research question clearly.
2. Define what evidence would support or refute it.
3. Identify data sources and feasibility.
4. Size the opportunity:
   - Affected population (how many players?)
   - Potential impact (what's the upside if we act?)
   - Confidence level in the estimate
5. Present findings with explicit assumptions and limitations.
6. Recommend: pursue further, test, or deprioritize — with reasoning.
