# Deep Dive Analysis

Structured, multi-section investigation into a specific topic. Deep dives are thorough — they take time, cover multiple angles, and produce a document that can stand on its own.

## Methodology

### 1. Scope & Frame
- Define the question clearly. What are we trying to understand or decide?
- Identify the primary audience and what decision this will inform.
- List the sub-questions that need answering.
- Set boundaries: what's in scope and what's explicitly not.

### 2. Prior Work Check
- Search `context/past-analysis/` for related prior work.
- Check if there's an existing framework or analysis that can be extended rather than rebuilt.

### 3. Data Exploration
- Start broad: pull top-level metrics to understand the landscape.
- Identify the dimensions that matter most for this topic.
- Look for surprises in the data before going deep on any one thread.

### 4. Analytical Sections
Structure the analysis as a series of questions, each with:
- The question being answered
- The query/approach used
- The finding (with data)
- The implication

### 5. Synthesis
- Connect the individual findings into a coherent narrative.
- Identify the 2-3 most important insights.
- Call out what surprised you vs. confirmed expectations.

### 6. Recommendations
- Specific, actionable, tied to team levers.
- Prioritized: what should we do first?
- Include what we still don't know and how to find out.

## Output Format
1. **Executive summary** (3-5 bullets: what we looked at, what we found, what to do)
2. **Context & scope** (why this matters, what's in/out of scope)
3. **Key findings** (each as a section with data + insight)
4. **Synthesis** (connecting the dots)
5. **Recommendations** (prioritized, actionable)
6. **Appendix** (detailed data tables, methodology notes)

## Guidelines
- A deep dive should take 4-8 queries minimum. If it's fewer, it's probably an ad hoc question.
- Always compare to benchmarks and historical norms.
- Segment aggressively — the insight is usually in the segment, not the average.
- Visualize cross-segment comparisons with heatmaps or grouped bars.
