**Coverage:** Mid-2023 through ~Q1 2025. May not reflect recent mechanic changes.
**Caveat:** Sentiment ratios are approximate estimates based on volume/tone, not exact measurements.

## 1. Adventures (Social, Team-Based)

**Overall Sentiment:** Mixed-to-Positive (improving over versions)

### Pain Points
- **Inactive teammates** is #1 complaint (~60% of Adventures threads contain this). "Got matched with 3 people who did absolutely nothing."
- Team composition feels random and unfair — wildly inconsistent experiences.
- Token earn rate from tournaments/milestones insufficient for F2P completion. "I play every tournament and still can't finish Adventures unless I buy tokens."
- No way to remove/replace inactive teammates.
- Rewards are back-loaded — big payoffs at the end most F2P teams never reach.
- Adventures V1 was poorly received; V2/V3 dramatically improved.

### Strengths
- Team element creates community when it works: "Most fun event when your team actually participates."
- V2/V3 card mechanics and mini-milestones praised specifically.
- Intermediate milestones provide steady gratification.
- Themed boards feel fresh.
- Considered best social event for casual players — lower-pressure than Racers, more engaging than Coop.

### F2P vs Payer
- **F2P:** Frequently can't complete; estimated ~10–20% completion. Still enjoy partial progress with active team.
- **Light spender ($5–$15):** One token pack makes meaningful difference. "I'll buy the starter bundle because it actually helps."
- **Whale:** Complete easily regardless of team. Some frustrated at subsidizing free teammates.

### Veteran vs New
- Veterans optimize tournament token earning, have friend groups for teaming → higher satisfaction.
- New players don't understand token pipeline: "I didn't know I had to play tournaments to get adventure tokens."

### Top Requests
- Choose/invite teammates (friends-only teams)
- Kick/replace inactive members mid-event
- Activity-based matchmaking
- Increase F2P token earn rate
- More intermediate milestones
- Solo path option

## 2. Coop / Partners (Social, 2-Player)

**Overall Sentiment:** Mixed (~55% negative, ~45% positive)

### Pain Points
- **Carry partner problem** is dominant complaint (~70% of negative posts). "My partner did 200 points and I did 78,000."
- No accountability — inactive partners still receive shared rewards.
- Matchmaking quality wildly inconsistent.
- Can't choose your own partner (top feature request).
- 80,000 points per attraction is near-impossible to solo when partner goes inactive.
- Repeated bad experiences cause event avoidance: "I don't even bother with Coop anymore."

### Strengths
- Highly satisfying when it works: "Had an amazing partner — we finished all 4 attractions with a day to spare."
- Shared progress creates motivating dynamic.
- Milestone rewards at attraction completion feel substantial.
- Simpler than Adventures — clearer contribution tracking.

### F2P vs Payer
- **F2P:** Most vocal complainers. Estimated completion ~15–25% (highly partner-dependent).
- **Light spender ($3–$10):** One token purchase helps but insufficient to solo carry.
- **Whale ($20+):** Can solo-complete but frustrated at subsidizing inactive partners.
- Aligns with **PAT-003**: "pushers" (~3% of regulars, ~5% of revenue) showing declining retention/ARPDAU.

### Veteran vs New
- Veterans have partner networks via Discord/Reddit → much better experience.
- New players lack networks, don't know how to earn tokens efficiently → uniformly negative first experiences.

### Top Requests
- **Let players choose their partner** (single most requested Coop feature)
- Minimum activity requirement for matching
- Re-match if partner inactive after X hours
- Reduce requirements when partner is inactive
- Show partner activity level before matching
- Proportional rewards based on contribution
- In-game chat/ping for partners

## 3. Racers / Tycoon Race (Social, Competitive)

**Overall Sentiment:** Mixed-to-Negative (~60% negative)

### Pain Points
- **Bracket matchmaking perceived as heavily unfair** (~65% of complaints). "I'm in a bracket with someone who has 50,000 points and I have 3,000."
- **Strongest P2W perception** of any minigame: "Racers is just a spending contest."
- Rewards too top-heavy — only top 3–5 get meaningful prizes.
- Encourages unhealthy play patterns (binge-rolling, staying up late).
- End-of-event sniping: "Got bumped from 3rd to 7th in the last 10 minutes."
- Bracket manipulation/sandbagging.
- High roll consumption with uncertain return.

### Strengths
- Competition is exciting when brackets are fair.
- Top rewards among best in game.
- Integrates with main gameplay naturally.
- Short duration (1–3 days).

### F2P vs Payer
- **F2P overwhelmingly negative.** "As a F2P player, Racers is just something I have to sit through." ~25% positive sentiment.
- **Light spenders still struggle** against heavy spenders.
- **Whales are the primary audience.** But whale-vs-whale brackets generate their own complaints.
- **F2P-to-payer experience gap is widest of any minigame.**

### Top Requests
- Fair matchmaking (by spending level, not just activity)
- Meaningful rewards through top 50%, not just top 3
- Anti-sandbagging measures
- Lock positions in final X minutes
- Casual bracket option for F2P
- Cap on rolls/points that can be used

## 4. Treasure Dig (Solo, Grid Excavation)

**Overall Sentiment:** Mixed-to-Positive (~55% positive)

### Pain Points
- **Perceived rigged RNG** on vault placement (~40% of complaints). "Vault is somehow always in the last 2 tiles."
- Multi-layer boards have very high token costs.
- Token cost per dig too high relative to earn rate.
- Low-value tiles feel like wasted digs.
- F2P completion wall at ~60–80%.

### Strengths
- **Satisfying "reveal" mechanic** — scratch-off appeal. "Feels like opening presents every time."
- Solo play removes social friction — progress is entirely personal.
- Strategic element in choosing dig locations.
- Clear visual progress.
- Vault reveals create genuine excitement.
- Perceived as one of the fairest minigames.

### F2P vs Payer
- **F2P** like it but frustrated by completion barrier (most complete 50–75%).
- **Light spender ($5):** One token pack often enables completion. "The $4.99 pack usually gets me to 100%."
- **Whale:** Easy, low-challenge. Low engagement ceiling.

### Top Requests
- Reduce token cost (especially deeper layers)
- Show remaining vault/chest count
- Increase base token earn rate
- Free daily dig bonus
- Reduce low-value tiles
- Hint system for vault locations

## 5. Boutique (Solo, Crafting/Orders)

**Overall Sentiment:** Mixed (~50/50, polarizing)

### Pain Points
- **Ingredient RNG** is frustrating. "I need red and the game keeps giving blue."
- Block mechanics feel designed to force purchases.
- Complexity high relative to other minigames.
- Progress feels slow.
- Ingredient-to-order ratio feels off.

### Strengths
- Crafting mechanic is engaging for optimization-minded players.
- Satisfying completion loop (orders → levels).
- Strategic depth in order prioritization.
- Themed aesthetics praised.
- A/B testing has visibly improved the event over iterations.

### F2P vs Payer
- **F2P:** Wide outcome range, estimated completion ~20–35%.
- **Light spender:** One ingredient/token pack often unblocks stalled progress.
- **Whale:** Brute-force through blocks. Less frustration but less engagement.

### Veteran vs New
- Veterans optimize order selection → higher satisfaction.
- New players find Boutique confusing — steepest learning curve of any minigame.

### Top Requests
- Reduce ingredient RNG / allow conversion
- Simplify order system
- Clearer completion path visibility
- Ingredient storage across events
- Better tutorial

## 6. Plinko / Prize Drop (Solo, Drop Game)

**Overall Sentiment:** Positive (~65% positive — most well-liked minigame)

### Pain Points
- Jackpot slot perceived as nearly impossible (~50% of complaints).
- Physics feel predetermined: "Drop position doesn't seem to matter."
- Low-value edge slots feel like wasted tokens.
- High token consumption rate.

### Strengths
- **Visually satisfying and fun to watch** — most consistently praised aspect. "It's like a mini game show."
- Simple, no-thought-required. "After a stressful day I just want to drop balls."
- Big-win excitement: "Hit the 5,000 roll slot and literally screamed."
- Quick sessions.
- Progressive milestone track ensures progress even with bad luck.
- Most accessible to all player types.

### F2P vs Payer
- **F2P enjoy it most** relative to other minigames — per-drop fun even without completion.
- **Light spender:** Good value, token packs = immediate gratification.
- **Whale:** RNG-heavy means spending more doesn't guarantee jackpots.

### Veteran vs New
- Equally accessible. New players often cite Prize Drop as favorite first event.

### Top Requests
- **Pity timer** guaranteeing a high-value hit after X drops
- Increase jackpot probability
- More player agency in aiming
- Show actual probability percentages
- Free daily drop bonus

## 7. Carnival Games (Solo, Rotating)

**Duration:** 1–2 days (shorter). Shared carnival token currency.

### Juggle Jam — Mixed-to-Negative (~55% negative)
- Input lag frustrating on mobile. Difficulty ramp too steep.
- Skill-based mechanic feels out of place in a resource management game.
- Positive: genuinely different, refreshing for competitive players.

### Battleship — Mixed-to-Positive (~55% positive)
- Matchmaking times too long; disconnections waste tokens.
- Positive: familiar nostalgic mechanic, strategic, satisfying board-clear.

### Fortune Teller — Mixed (~50/50)
- Feels entirely RNG-based: "Just a lottery with extra steps."
- Positive: mystery/reveal mechanic inherently engaging, polished aesthetics.

### F2P vs Payer (all Carnival)
- **F2P view Carnival as most accessible** — shorter duration, lower token requirements. "Carnival events are the ones I can actually finish."
- **Payers find rewards underwhelming** — "Not worth buying tokens for a 1-day event."
- F2P/payer gap is narrower here than major minigames.

### Top Requests
- Improve Juggle Jam input responsiveness
- Fix Battleship matchmaking/disconnections
- Add strategy elements to Fortune Teller
- Extend to 2 days
- "Carnival Pass" across rotating sub-games

## Cross-Cutting Themes

### Tournament/Milestone Grind (~70% negative)
- Double-currency drain frustration: "Spend rolls → earn tournament points → earn tokens → play event → earn rewards including... rolls. Hamster wheel."
- Tournament fatigue from near-continuous tournaments.
- Milestone later nodes require more points than first 20 combined.
- **Rush/Flag events are the meta:** "Save all rolls for flags or you're doing it wrong." Creates feast/famine play.

### Matchmaking (~75% negative for social events)
- Coop carry-partner: single most discussed issue.
- Adventures inactive teammates: less intense (1 in 4 vs 1 in 2).
- Racers bracket spending gap.
- **Unifying request: let players choose teammates/partners.**

### Event Fatigue (~65% experiencing)
- Too many overlapping events: "I have 5 running right now."
- FOMO-driven engagement feels coercive.
- No downtime between events → burnout cycles (2–3 weeks hard play → month off).
- Counter: ~35% love always having something to do.

### Economy / Roll Scarcity (~60% negative)
- Rolls bottleneck everything. Scarcity cascades through entire game.
- Regeneration feels slow (burns in 2 minutes at 10x).
- Multiplier trap: 1x feels pointless, 10x destroys rolls.
- **Post-June 2025 improvement noticed and appreciated** (aligns with PAT-006).

### Progressive Offers (~55% negative)
- Recognized as psychological tactic: "Classic dark pattern."
- FOMO on next tier after first buy.
- Creates spending regret.
- Whales see through it but buy anyway for best value.

## Strategic Takeaways (for quantitative follow-up)

1. **Social events have highest ceiling and lowest floor.** Variance in experience is the problem, not the concept.
2. **Player agency over partner/team selection** is the most wanted feature across all social minigames.
3. **Plinko/Prize Drop is the safest design** — highest positive sentiment, lowest frustration, good across all player types.
4. **Racers has strongest P2W perception** — F2P have essentially given up competing. May serve whale engagement at cost of broader satisfaction.
5. **Coop carry-partner problem is a retention risk** — PAT-003 confirms: pushers (~3% regulars, ~5% revenue) with declining retention/ARPDAU.
6. **Token economy frustration is upstream of all minigame frustration.** Rolls → tokens pipeline efficiency is the master lever.
7. **Event fatigue approaching critical mass.** The ~35% who love always-on may mask deteriorating broader sentiment.
8. **Post-June 2025 economy improvements were noticed** — further VfM changes would lift sentiment across all minigames (PAT-006).
